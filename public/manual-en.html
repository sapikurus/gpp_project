1. Overview

GPP Portal is the internal trading operations system for PT Global Petro Pasifik. It covers the full fuel trading cycle — from supply procurement and stock management through pricing calculation, offer letters, sales orders, purchase orders, and delivery documentation.

What GPP Portal Does

ModulePurpose
StokTrack incoming cargo positions and supply tranches from multiple suppliers
CalculatorReal-time P&L modeling — blended cost, cost of money, margin analysis
Surat PenawaranGenerate formal offer letters to clients with full tax breakdown
Sales OrderFormalize client commitments linked to offering letters and stock
Purchase OrderIssue procurement orders to suppliers with approval workflow
Delivery OrderIssue delivery documentation and bill of delivery receipts
Data MOPSLive MOPS pricing, JISDOR, bi-monthly Pertamina reference data
Master DataCompany profiles, client database, suppliers, products, tax rates

Design Principles

  • PPN is pass-through — VAT is never included in profit calculations. It is collected from clients and remitted to the government; GPP claims input VAT against output VAT.
  • Blended cost is volume-weighted — when a cargo has multiple supply tranches at different prices, the calculator blends them proportionally by volume.
  • All approvals are role-gated — documents flow through a configurable approval chain before becoming final.
  • No data is lost on navigation — critical data (clients, suppliers) saves immediately to Firestore when entered.

2. Getting Started

Logging In

Navigate to gpp-project.vercel.app (or your custom domain).

Enter your email and password. Your account must be created in Firebase Authentication by the administrator.

After logging in, your role is loaded automatically from the database. You will see only the menus your role allows.

ℹ️

First-time login: contact your administrator to create your Firebase account and assign your role in Master Data → Settings → Users & Roles. Until a role is assigned, you default to Staff.

Changing Your Password

Click 🔑 Ganti Password in the bottom-left sidebar.

Enter your current password, then your new password (minimum 6 characters), then confirm it.

Click Ubah Password. A ✅ confirmation appears. Your password is changed immediately.

Firebase re-authentication is required — if your current password is wrong you will see an error.

Installing as a Mobile App (PWA)

GPP Portal is a Progressive Web App. On mobile:

  • Android (Chrome): tap the menu (⋮) → "Add to Home Screen"
  • iOS (Safari): tap the Share icon → "Add to Home Screen"

Once installed, GPP Portal opens like a native app without a browser bar.

The Dashboard is the first screen after login. It provides an at-a-glance summary of operations and alerts you to any documents waiting for your approval.

Pending Approval Tasks

The amber notification panel at the top shows all documents waiting for your specific role's approval. Each item shows:

  • Document type (color-coded: purple = PO, blue = SO, amber = OL)
  • Document number and vendor/client name
  • Current status badge (e.g., "Menunggu Manager")
  • Buka → button — navigates directly to that module
✅

If there are no pending tasks, this panel is hidden. A clean dashboard means no action is required.

4. Stok (Cargo Positions)

The Stok module tracks incoming supply positions. Each position can hold multiple tranches — separate shipments from different suppliers, vessels, or purchase dates that form one combined cargo.

Understanding the Layout

  • Left panel: List of all cargo positions. Click any card to view its detail.
  • Right panel: Selected position's stats, tranche table, and linked POs.

Creating a New Position

Click + New Position in the left panel.

Enter a descriptive label (e.g., "Batam P1 MAY 2026"), product type, loading port, discharging port, and ETA dates.

Click + Create Position. The stock is created in Draft status with no tranches yet.

Adding a Tranche

With a stock selected, click + Add Tranche in the top-right of the detail panel.

Fill in the tranche details:

  • Supplier — who you're buying from
  • Vessel / SPOB — the delivery vessel for this specific tranche
  • Base Price (IDR/L) — purchase price before taxes
  • Volume (L) — quantity in litres
  • Load Date — when product is loaded/received
  • Pay Date — when payment is made to supplier (used for CoM)
  • Province — supplier's province (determines PBBKB rate)

Select applicable taxes from the supplier:

  • PPN — check this if the supplier charges VAT. Note: PPN does NOT affect the Modal/L or profit calculation — it is pass-through.
  • BPH Migas — check if supplier charges this levy (0.25%)
  • PBBKB — check if supplier charges fuel tax for their province. Check "Omit PBBKB" if supplier is non-Wapu or non-PKP.

Optionally, link a Purchase Order (see below). Click 💾 Save Tranche.

⚠️

PPN is pass-through. Even if the supplier charges PPN 11%, the system excludes it from the Modal/L and blended cost calculation. This is correct — GPP claims the input VAT back against the output VAT charged to clients.

Linking a PO to a Tranche

Once a Purchase Order has been created, you can link it to the relevant tranche to validate prices and volumes:

Edit an existing tranche (hover the row → click Edit).

In the "Link ke Purchase Order" section, select the PO from the dropdown.

The system compares tranche price and volume against the PO items. You will see either:

  • ✅ Harga dan volume sesuai PO — all good
  • ⚠️ Tidak sesuai PO — with exact differences shown. Use ↓ Prefill dari PO to sync.

Save the tranche. The stock automatically moves to Confirmed status when any tranche has a linked PO.

ℹ️

Workflow tip: Traders often model tranches before committing to a PO. You can create a stock with tranches for quick P&L calculation, then create the PO (which triggers the approval workflow), and link it back to the tranche once approved. The tranche fields are never locked — they remain editable even after PO linking.

5. Calculator

The Calculator is the core P&L modeling tool. It computes real-time margin analysis for a given cargo position and selling scenario.

Loading a Cargo Position

Click Prefill dari Stok (or navigate from the Stok module via the 🧮 Kalkulasi button) to load a cargo's tranches automatically.

Alternatively, manually enter tranches in the Cargo Position panel on the left.

Setting the Sell Price

Two sell modes are available:

  • Direct — enter the agreed sell price (IDR/L) directly.
  • MOPS Formula — the sell price is calculated from: (MOPS × JISDOR / 158.987 + Premium) × Weight% + HIP BBN × (1 - Weight%). Useful for MOPS-indexed contracts.

Understanding the P&L Output

LineDescription
Blended Buy (Modal)Volume-weighted average effective cost per litre (excl. PPN)
Cost of Money (CoM)Financing cost from load date to payment date. Compound: Modal × ((1 + rate)^(days/365) − 1)
LossVolume loss tolerance on sell price. Sell × loss%
Fuel MarginSell − Modal − CoM − Loss
OAT ModalYour freight/logistics cost per litre
OAT ClientFreight charged to client per litre
Freight MarginOAT Client − OAT Modal
Total MarginFuel Margin + Freight Margin

Client Invoice Section

Below the P&L table, the calculator shows the client invoice breakdown:

  • DPP (Dasar Pengenaan Pajak) — the sell price per litre, base for tax calculation
  • PBBKB — fuel tax charged to client (if your company is registered in that province)
  • PPN 11% — output VAT charged to client (pass-through)
  • OAT Client — freight charge
  • Client Pays — total per litre the client actually pays

Saving Snapshots

Click 💾 Simpan Kalkulasi to save the current calculation as an immutable snapshot. Snapshots can be recalled later for reference or to continue editing — they include all cargo, pricing, and result data.

6. Surat Penawaran (Offering Letters)

The Offering Letter module generates formal price offer letters (Surat Penawaran Harga / SPH) to clients.

List View

The main page shows all issued letters in a table with: No. Surat | Tanggal | Client | Periode | DPP/L | Status. Hover a row to reveal action buttons: Approval | Edit | Print | Del.

Creating a New Letter

Click + New Letter. A full-page form opens.

The Sequence No. field auto-fills with the next number. You can override it using the − / + stepper. The document number preview updates live in the header.

Select a Client from the dropdown. Client details (name, code, address) are pulled from Master Data automatically.

The Period auto-fills with the current bi-monthly Pertamina pricing period (e.g., "1 - 14 Mei 2026"). Edit it if needed.

Select Province (PBBKB) — this is the client's delivery province, which determines whether PBBKB applies on the sell side.

Set Terms of Payment: Credit (with TOP days) · CBD · COD · Lainnya (free text).

In the Price column, enter the DPP/L and select applicable taxes:

  • PPN — output VAT (11%). Charged to client, pass-through to tax authority.
  • PPH — income tax (0.3%). Becomes a cost to the buyer.
  • PBBKB — fuel tax. Check this, and the rate for the selected province appears. Only applies if GPP is registered (✓) in that province.

Add Transport Sites (OAT) if applicable — each site has a name and IDR/L rate charged to the client.

Click 📄 Buat Surat to save. The letter is saved in Draft status.

Document Number Format

001/SPH/GPP/PTRO/V/2026

  • 001 — 3-digit sequence (editable)
  • SPH — fixed identifier
  • GPP — entity
  • PTRO — client code (set in Master Data → Client)
  • V — month in Roman numerals
  • 2026 — year

Computer Generated Option

Enable "Computer Generated — No Signature Required" in the Opsi Dokumen card. When printing, the signature block is replaced with a statement that the document was generated electronically and is valid without a wet signature.

Printing

Hover a row in the list and click 🖨️. A print-ready preview opens with the official letter format, GPP letterhead, price table, OAT table, and payment terms. Documents in Draft or pending status show a "DRAFT" diagonal watermark.

7. Sales Order

A Sales Order (SO) formalizes a client's commitment to purchase. It links to an Offering Letter (optional) and reserves stock volume.

Creating a Sales Order

Click + SO Baru.

Optionally select a Surat Penawaran from the dropdown. This auto-fills: client name, product, agreed price (DPP), delivery province, and payment terms from the OL.

Select a Stok — only Confirmed stocks appear, with available volume shown.

Enter the volume, agreed price, delivery location, and payment terms.

Click + Buat SO.

SO and Stock Volume

When a Sales Order receives final approval (last approver in the chain signs off), the system runs a Firestore transaction that:

  1. Sets the SO status to approved
  2. Adds the SO volume to the stock's committedVolume
  3. If committed ≥ total volume, marks the stock as Sold Out
⚠️

The system warns if an SO volume exceeds available stock — but it does not block you from submitting. The approver should verify availability before approving.

Linking SO to an OL

The OL reference appears in the SO card (📄 001/SPH/GPP/...) and in the Dashboard recent SO list. This creates a traceable chain: OL → SO → Stock deduction.

8. Purchase Order

Purchase Orders are issued to suppliers for fuel procurement. Every PO goes through the approval chain before it is considered binding.

Creating a PO

Click + New Entry.

Select a supplier from the dropdown (auto-fills name, address, NPWP from Master Data), or enter manually.

Add line items: description, quantity, unit, unit price, discount. Multiple items are supported.

In Pajak & Pungutan, select applicable taxes: PPH, BPH Migas, PBBKB (with province).

The Ringkasan sidebar updates live with subtotals and total.

Click + Buat PO. The PO is saved in Draft — it must be submitted for approval before it is considered binding.

PO Approval

Hover a PO row in the list → click Approval. This opens the approval panel where you can submit, approve, or reject the document based on your role.

Linking PO to Tranche

After a PO is approved, edit the corresponding tranche in the Stok module and link the PO. The system then shows a ✅/⚠️ match indicator comparing PO price and volume against the tranche's recorded values.

9. Delivery Order

The Delivery Order module tracks product deliveries and generates:

  • Surat Jalan (Delivery Note) — accompanies the physical delivery
  • BDR (Berita Acara Delivery Receipt) — confirmation of receipt

Document numbers follow the format: 001/DO-GPP/V/2026 and 001/BDR-GPP/V/2026.

11. Master Data

Master Data is the configuration hub for the system. It is only accessible to Directors and Super Admins.

Corporate

Configure GPP's own company profile:

  • General Info — company name, head office address, NPWP, signatories
  • Branches — add unlimited branch offices, each with their own NPWP
  • Bank Info — multiple bank accounts; mark one as primary (★)
  • Rates & PBBKB — set global tax rates (PPN %, PPH %, BPH Migas %, Bank Rate for CoM); configure province-by-province PBBKB rates and toggle GPP's registration status per province
⚠️

Click 💾 Simpan at the top of the Corporate section to save changes. Corporate data uses a "save all" approach — changes accumulate until you save.

Client Database

Clients appear as dropdowns in Surat Penawaran and Sales Order.

Click + New Entry to open the client form.

Fill in: Company Name, Client Code (e.g., PTRO — used in the OL document number), NPWP, Address (multi-line, use +/− to control rows — each line appears separately in the offer letter), Contact/PIC, Email, TOP Default (days).

Click 💾 Simpan. The entry is saved immediately to Firestore — navigating away will not lose it.

ℹ️

Client Code is critical. It becomes part of the offering letter document number (e.g., 001/SPH/GPP/PTRO/V/2026). Use a short, consistent code for each client.

Supplier Database

Suppliers appear in the Purchase Order form. The same card-based pattern applies: click + New Entry, fill the form (name, NPWP, address, PIC, email, phone), and click 💾 Simpan. Data saves immediately.

Settings

The Settings tab (Director/Super Admin only) contains three sub-sections:

Users & Roles

Add a user's email address and assign their role. Roles take effect immediately on next login (or page refresh). To add a new user account, go to Firebase Console → Authentication → Add user first, then assign the role here.

Approval Chain Configuration

For each document type (PO, SO, Surat Penawaran), select which roles must approve it:

  • ☑ Manager + ☑ Director → two-level approval (default)
  • ☐ Manager + ☑ Director → director-only (single level)

Changes take effect immediately for new documents. Documents already in the approval chain continue with their original chain.

MOPS Endpoint & Backup

  • Configure the GAS Web App URL for MOPS data
  • Export a full JSON backup of all data
  • Import / restore from a backup (counters are never reset by import)

13. Approval Workflow

GPP Portal uses a configurable multi-level approval system for Purchase Orders, Sales Orders, and Offering Letters.

Approval States

StatusMeaningWho Can Act
DraftCreated, not submittedCreator can edit and submit
Menunggu ManagerSubmitted, awaiting managerManager (or Director)
Menunggu DirekturManager approved, awaiting directorDirector (or Super Admin)
DisetujuiFully approvedImmutable (cannot be edited)
DitolakRejected at any levelReturns to Draft for correction

The Approval Flow

Staff/any role creates a document in Draft status.

They open the document's Approval panel and click 📤 Ajukan untuk Persetujuan. Status moves to "Menunggu Manager".

The Manager sees the document in the Dashboard pending tasks or navigates to the module. They open the Approval panel, optionally add a note, and click ✓ Setujui. Status moves to "Menunggu Direktur".

The Director repeats the same process. On approval, the document becomes Disetujui. For Sales Orders, stock is deducted at this point via a Firestore transaction.

Rejection

Either approver can reject a document. They must enter a rejection note. The document returns to Draft with the rejection reason displayed. The creator can edit and resubmit.

DRAFT Watermark

Any document printed before receiving final approval shows a large diagonal "DRAFT" watermark. This is removed automatically once the document is fully approved.

14. Key Concepts

Blended Modal (Blended Buy Price)

When a cargo has multiple supply tranches at different prices, the blended modal is the volume-weighted average of all effective costs:

Blended Modal = Σ(effective cost/L × volume) / Σ(volume)

Where effective cost/L = Base Price + PBBKB + BPH Migas (PPN excluded).

Cost of Money (CoM)

CoM represents the financing cost of holding inventory. It uses compound interest:

CoM = Modal × ((1 + bank_rate)^(days/365) − 1)

  • Days = from weighted average load date to payment date (offering date + client TOP)
  • Bank Rate = configured in Master Data → Corporate → Rates (e.g., 6.5% p.a.)

PPN — Pass-Through Tax

PPN (Pajak Pertambahan Nilai / VAT) at 11% is never a cost to GPP:

  • GPP pays PPN to suppliers → recorded as input tax
  • GPP charges PPN to clients → recorded as output tax
  • Net VAT = output − input → remitted to the tax authority
  • GPP's profit margin is unaffected by PPN

In the system, the PPN checkbox on tranches is for documentation only. It does not affect Modal/L or the blended buy price.

PBBKB — Pajak Bahan Bakar Kendaraan Bermotor

PBBKB is a provincial fuel tax, typically 5–7.5%. It applies differently on buy and sell sides:

  • Buy side (tranche): If the supplier charges PBBKB for their province, it IS a real cost (added to Modal/L). Can be omitted if supplier is non-Wapu.
  • Sell side (client invoice): GPP charges PBBKB to the client only if GPP is registered (✓) in the delivery province. Configure registration status in Master Data → Corporate → Rates & PBBKB.

Bi-Monthly Pertamina Pricing Period

Pertamina updates its published prices (HIP BBN) twice a month:

  • Period 1: 1st – 14th of each month
  • Period 2: 15th – last day of each month

The system auto-fills the current period in Surat Penawaran, but it is always editable.
